Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Research Neuroplasticity

Learning about neuroplasticity has shown me that my brain is capable of change and growth. It means I’m not stuck with my current abilities—consistent effort leads to real improvement. I’ll use this by regularly challenging myself with new coding problems, reviewing concepts more deeply, and focusing on truly understanding content, not just completing tasks. Small habits like regular breaks, sleep, and staying curious will also support my learning.

Research Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is the belief that abilities can be developed through effort, feedback, and learning from mistakes. This is especially important in programming, where failure is part of the process. I used to focus too much on just getting tasks done, but now I see value in taking time to learn properly. I’ll work on asking for help earlier and treating setbacks as part of my progress.

Blending with My Learning Plan

These ideas fit well with my learning plan. Neuroplasticity supports my goal of becoming a software developer by reminding me that effort and repetition build skill. Growth mindset connects with my aim to improve communication and teamwork—I’ll ask for help when needed and contribute more openly. I’ll also reflect more often on what I’ve learned and keep reminding myself that growth takes time and persistence.

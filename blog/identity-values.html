Identity, Values, and Strengths

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I read this question, I remembered a time a while ago when someone in my friend group posted a disrespectful photo of another friend onto social media with a rude caption. My other friends found it funny, but I knew it wasn’t right. I asked the person to take it down, which they did. I was nervous about causing drama, but I chose to stand up for kindness and respect. Looking back, I’m glad I did as it showed me it is important to stand up for others, even when it can be uncomfortable.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

During my time growing up, my mother and father have been committed to the local Anglican church, and I grew up going to church with them. During this time, I was influenced into Christian-like values like respect, compassion, forgiveness, honesty, and kindness and these values have played a powerful role into shaping my identity.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

A few years ago, I took a Clifton Strengths Finder online for preparation for a 2-week Christian camp I went on. My top 5 strengths were harmony, restorative, analytical, responsibility, and focus. During this camp I really learnt about how I can use these strengths to work well with others and learn more about myself. These strengths can help me in my learning journey by helping me stay motivated, solve problems, and think deeply about what I’m studying. They also help me work well with others, stay organized, and take ownership of my goals.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

As an 18-year-old entering tertiary education, I recognize that I have some limitations compared to older students who may have more life experience or work history. This difference might make it challenging to relate to certain discussions or feel as confident in professional settings, which could affect my participation and networking opportunities. Additionally, older students might have clearer career goals or skills, so I may need extra time to explore and define my own path. However, being aware of these limitations helps me stay open to learning from others and motivated to develop my skills throughout my journey.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

During the 2-week Christian camp I mentioned earlier, I had the be leader of the day where I had to make the group decisions and make sure everyone was comfortable during the day. I had 2 different days to do this and during my first try I took an autocratic or controlling approach of leadership where I just made decisions independently and didn’t take the thoughts of my group into consideration, which caused tension in the group. On my second try, I took a democratic or team-orientated leadership style, where I listened to everyone’s ideas which encouraged innovation and diverse perspectives. This helped the group feel included and raised the team morale.

Nathan Johnson 2025.

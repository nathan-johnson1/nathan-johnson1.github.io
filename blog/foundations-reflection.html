Foundations Reflection

1. What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. I’ve noticed that I put a lot of pressure on myself to solve things alone, and I don’t always ask for help when I probably should, even when it would save time.
  2. Working solo has made me realise how easy it is to get stuck in my own head. I’m learning that collaboration is something I want to grow into, not avoid.
  3. I’ve started to reflect more on how I respond to challenge and frustration, and I’m hoping that as I begin working with others, I’ll be more open, curious, and receptive.

2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

They help build better collaboration. Programming is rarely solo, understanding others, being patient, and knowing how you respond under pressure makes teamwork smoother and more respectful.

3. What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Honestly, how relevant it is. I expected it to be separate from coding, but it’s been just as important, especially during pair programming or group work where communication is everything.

4. What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Slowing down to reflect was surprisingly challenging. I’m used to focusing on solving problems or writing code, so stepping back to think about mindset, habits, or how I react to challenges felt unfamiliar at first. It took some effort to be honest with myself.

5. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because technical skill isn’t enough in real-world teams. Projects succeed or fail based on communication, collaboration, and mindset, not just code.

6. Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not a waste at all. It made me a more balanced learner. Knowing JavaScript is great, but being able to explain my thinking, handle feedback, and work well with others is what makes me actually job-ready.

Nathan Johnson 2025.

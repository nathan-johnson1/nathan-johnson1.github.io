Emotional Intelligence

• What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize, understand, and manage your own emotions, as well as recognize and influence the emotions of others. It involves being aware of how you feel, controlling your reactions, staying motivated, showing empathy, and handling relationships well. People with strong emotional intelligence can navigate social situations more effectively and make better decisions, both in their personal lives and at work.

• How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ and IQ are different in what they measure and how they affect our lives. IQ refers to cognitive abilities like logical reasoning, problem-solving, and understanding complex ideas—it's mostly about how well your brain processes information. EQ, on the other hand, is about understanding and managing emotions, both your own and other people’s. While IQ might help you succeed in academic or technical tasks, EQ is crucial for building relationships, communicating effectively, and handling stress or conflict.

• Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it helps you navigate life’s challenges more effectively. It allows you to manage your emotions, stay calm under pressure, communicate clearly, and build strong, healthy relationships. This makes EQ a key factor not just in personal well-being, but also in professional success, often even more than IQ.
